1. What is RASH? Acronym for Research Article in Semplified HTML, RASH is a standardized HTML for scientific publications.

2. What is EasyRASH? It's overall a web application with which is possible to make annotations on RASH articles. In the scientific community articles must be approved before the effective publication. EasyRASH provides all the tools for a complete peer review process:
author writes the article and submit it to the review of other people. The reviewers can make annotations on the text and can provide a general judgement on the article. Finally there is the so-called chair, who can accept or reject the article.

3. Roles. There are mainly 3 roles:

  • Reader (passive user): can read the article
  • Annotator (active user): can make annotations and submit review
  • Chair (active user): expresses the final judgment (accepted or rejected)

EasyRASH manages all of them.

5. Allowed selections. When the annotator performs a selection on the text and tries to make an annotation on that fragment, it may occurs the selection not allowed error. This because not every parts of the document are annotable. Therefore let's list the parts of the document which can raise that error:

  • The header: the title, the informations about the authors, the keywords etc... are not annotable. (All parts before the abstract section).
  • The references and the footnotes at the bottom of the documents
  • A selection that includes 2 or more differents paragraphs
  • A selection that includes the section title and the following paragraph
  • A selection that split the links, the sentences (or words) in bold, the words in red and the anchors. If you want to annotate them, you must select the entire word/sentence.
  • A selection that includes one or more images.
  • A selection that includes the bold part in the captions

Most of theese limitations are due to the following cause: a lot of the parts of the RASH articles are generated on the client, so they can't be saved in the server, and all the annotations to the client-side generated parts, would be lost.
